## 5. Important Content Style Rules

This lesson is for Grade 3 learners. Do not use short abstract grammar definitions alone.

Avoid wording like:

- “An adjective describes a noun.”
- “An adverb modifies a verb.”
- “A preposition shows relationship.”
- “A conjunction connects clauses.”

Use child-friendly wording instead:

- “An adjective tells us more about a person, animal, place, or thing.”
- “An adverb tells us more about an action.”
- “A preposition tells us where something is.”
- “A conjunction is a joining word.”

Use these simple phrases consistently:

- naming word
- action word
- tells us more about
- used instead of a noun
- joining word
- tells us where something is
- shows strong feeling
- small word used before a noun

Every part of speech section must include:

1. A simple meaning
2. A visual hint or emoji examples
3. Easy Grade 3 examples
4. One short sentence
5. A clear explanation of which word is being taught
6. A mini practice activity

# 7. Full Lesson Content

## Lesson: Parts of Speech

### Lesson introduction

Words do different jobs in a sentence.

Some words name people, places, animals, or things.
Some words show actions.
Some words tell us more about other words.
Some words join ideas together.

These word groups are called **parts of speech**.

In this lesson, we shall learn:

1. Nouns
2. Pronouns
3. Verbs
4. Adjectives
5. Adverbs
6. Prepositions
7. Conjunctions
8. Interjections
9. Articles

---

## 7.1 Nouns

### Simple meaning

A **noun** is a **naming word**.

It names a:

- person
- place
- animal
- thing

### Visual examples

| Picture | Word | Type |
|---|---|---|
| 👦 | boy | person |
| 👩‍🏫 | teacher | person |
| 🏫 | school | place |
| 🐶 | dog | animal |
| 📘 | book | thing |

### Sentence example

The **boy** carries a **book** to **school**.

### Explanation

In this sentence:

- **boy** is a noun because it names a person.
- **book** is a noun because it names a thing.
- **school** is a noun because it names a place.

### Mini practice

Find the nouns in this sentence:

The **dog** sits near the **tree**.

Expected answer:

- dog
- tree

---

## 7.2 Pronouns

### Simple meaning

A **pronoun** is a word we use **instead of a noun**.

It helps us avoid repeating the same name again and again.

### Visual examples

| Noun | Pronoun |
|---|---|
| Mary | she |
| John | he |
| dog | it |
| children | they |
| my friend and I | we |

### Sentence example

**Mary** is reading. **She** is happy.

### Explanation

**She** is a pronoun.

It is used instead of saying **Mary** again.

### More examples

- Peter is running. **He** is fast.
- The dog is hungry. **It** wants food.
- The children are singing. **They** are happy.

### Mini practice

Choose the correct pronoun:

Amina is writing. ______ is using a pencil.

Options:

- He
- She
- They

Expected answer:

**She**

---

## 7.3 Verbs

### Simple meaning

A **verb** is an **action word**.

It tells us what someone or something **does**.

### Visual examples

| Picture | Verb |
|---|---|
| 🏃 | run |
| 🤸 | jump |
| 🍽️ | eat |
| ✍️ | write |
| 🎶 | sing |

### Sentence example

The boy **runs**.

### Explanation

**runs** is the verb because it tells us what the boy does.

### More examples

- The girl **jumps**.
- The baby **eats**.
- The teacher **writes**.
- The children **sing**.

### Mini practice

Find the verb in this sentence:

The girl **reads** a story.

Expected answer:

**reads**

---

## 7.4 Adjectives

### Simple meaning

An **adjective** tells us more about a **person, animal, place, or thing**.

It can tell us things like:

- color
- size
- shape
- feeling
- number
- how something looks

Do not teach it only as “an adjective describes a noun.” That wording is too abstract for many Grade 3 learners.

Use this wording:

**An adjective tells us more about a person, animal, place, or thing.**

### Visual examples

| Picture | Words | What the adjective tells us |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 | red ball | color |
| 🐘 | big elephant | size |
| 😊 | happy child | feeling |
| 🧼 | clean classroom | how it looks |
| 3️⃣ | three pencils | number |

### Sentence example

The **happy** girl has a **red** bag.

### Explanation

- **happy** tells us more about the girl.
- **red** tells us more about the bag.

### More examples

- a **big** dog
- a **small** cup
- a **clean** shirt
- a **round** ball
- **three** books

### Mini practice

Find the adjectives in this sentence:

The **small** boy has a **blue** pencil.

Expected answer:

- small
- blue

---

## 7.5 Adverbs

### Simple meaning

An **adverb** tells us more about an **action**.

It can tell us:

- how an action happens
- when an action happens
- where an action happens

Do not teach it as “an adverb modifies a verb.” That is too hard for Grade 3.

Use this wording:

**An adverb tells us more about an action.**

### Visual examples

| Question | Example | Sentence |
|---|---|---|
| How? | quickly | The boy runs **quickly**. |
| How? | slowly | The old man walks **slowly**. |
| How? | loudly | The baby cries **loudly**. |
| When? | today | We shall play **today**. |
| Where? | outside | The children are playing **outside**. |

### Sentence example

The girl reads **slowly**.

### Explanation

**slowly** is an adverb because it tells us how the girl reads.

### More examples

- The boy runs **quickly**.
- The baby cries **loudly**.
- We shall go **tomorrow**.
- The children are playing **outside**.

### Mini practice

Find the adverb in this sentence:

The rabbit jumps **quickly**.

Expected answer:

**quickly**

---

## 7.6 Prepositions

### Simple meaning

A **preposition** tells us **where something is**.

It can show position or place.

Do not teach it as “a preposition shows relationship.” That is too abstract for Grade 3.

Use this wording:

**A preposition tells us where something is.**

### Visual examples

| Picture idea | Preposition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 📦🐱 | in | The cat is **in** the box. |
| 🪑🐱 | on | The cat is **on** the chair. |
| 🪑⬇️🐱 | under | The cat is **under** the chair. |
| 🚪🐱 | behind | The cat is **behind** the door. |
| 🏠🌳 | near | The tree is **near** the house. |

### Sentence example

The cat is **under** the table.

### Explanation

**under** is a preposition because it tells us where the cat is.

### More examples

- The book is **on** the desk.
- The pencil is **in** the bag.
- The boy is **near** the door.
- The ball is **behind** the chair.

### Mini practice

Find the preposition in this sentence:

The pencil is **in** the box.

Expected answer:

**in**

---

## 7.7 Conjunctions

### Simple meaning

A **conjunction** is a **joining word**.

It joins words or sentences.

### Visual examples

| Conjunction | What it does | Example |
|---|---|---|
| and | joins things together | tea **and** bread |
| but | joins different ideas | I am small **but** strong. |
| or | shows a choice | Do you want milk **or** juice? |
| because | gives a reason | I slept because I was tired. |

### Sentence example

I like mangoes **and** bananas.

### Explanation

**and** is a conjunction because it joins **mangoes** and **bananas**.

### More examples

- I can read **and** write.
- I wanted to play **but** it rained.
- You can use a pen **or** a pencil.
- I drank water **because** I was thirsty.

### Mini practice

Find the conjunction in this sentence:

I like rice **and** beans.

Expected answer:

**and**

---

## 7.8 Interjections

### Simple meaning

An **interjection** is a word that shows a **strong feeling**.

It can show:

- surprise
- joy
- pain
- excitement
- worry

### Visual examples

| Feeling | Interjection | Example |
|---|---|---|
| surprise | Wow! | **Wow!** That balloon is big. |
| mistake | Oops! | **Oops!** I dropped my pencil. |
| joy | Hurray! | **Hurray!** We won the game. |
| pain | Ouch! | **Ouch!** My foot hurts. |
| sadness | Oh no! | **Oh no!** It is raining. |

### Sentence example

**Wow!** That cake is big.

### Explanation

**Wow!** is an interjection because it shows surprise.

### Mini practice

Find the interjection in this sentence:

**Oops!** I spilled the water.

Expected answer:

**Oops!**

---

## 7.9 Articles

### Simple meaning

An **article** is a small word used before a noun.

The common articles are:

- a
- an
- the

### Visual examples

| Article | Example | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| a | a dog | use before one dog |
| an | an orange | use before words that begin with a vowel sound |
| the | the sun | use when we are talking about something known |

### Sentence examples

I saw **a** dog.

She ate **an** orange.

**The** sun is hot.

### Explanation

- **a** comes before the noun **dog**.
- **an** comes before the noun **orange**.
- **the** comes before the noun **sun**.

### Mini practice

Choose the correct article:

I ate ______ apple.

Options:

- a
- an
- the

Expected answer:

**an**

# 10. Mixed Examples for Learners

Include these examples to help learners see different parts of speech in one sentence.

## Example 1

Sentence:

The **dog** runs **quickly**.

Explanation:

- **dog** is a noun because it names an animal.
- **runs** is a verb because it shows an action.
- **quickly** is an adverb because it tells us more about the action.

---

## Example 2

Sentence:

**She** has a **blue** dress.

Explanation:

- **She** is a pronoun because it is used instead of a name.
- **blue** is an adjective because it tells us more about the dress.
- **dress** is a noun because it names a thing.

---

## Example 3

Sentence:

**Wow!** The cat is **under** the table.

Explanation:

- **Wow!** is an interjection because it shows surprise.
- **cat** is a noun because it names an animal.
- **under** is a preposition because it tells us where the cat is.
- **table** is a noun because it names a thing.

---

## Example 4

Sentence:

I ate **an** orange **and** a banana.

Explanation:

- **I** is a pronoun.
- **ate** is a verb because it shows an action.
- **an** is an article.
- **orange** is a noun.
- **and** is a conjunction because it joins orange and banana.
- **banana** is a noun.
