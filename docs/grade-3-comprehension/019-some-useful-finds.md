# Grade 3 English Comprehension Exam 019: Some Useful Finds

## Teacher Notes

- This passage has been rewritten and expanded for Grade 3 learners.
- Questions test reading comprehension, vocabulary in context, inference, sequencing, values, and light spelling recognition.
- The questions avoid sounding like grammar drills while still strengthening language skills.

## Passage

James, Jenny, and Penny were walking along a quiet lane when the rubbish dump came into sight. It looked like a low, flat hill made of ashes and cinders. Old tyres, broken chairs, bent tins, and pieces of wood lay all around.

James and Jenny ran towards it as if they had found a secret treasure island. They climbed over the rubbish carefully, calling out with excitement each time they found something useful.

Penny followed more slowly. She did not like the grey dust that rose under her shoes, and she disliked the crunching sound of the cinders beneath her feet. Still, she kept looking.

Soon Penny found a big bundle of paper. One side of each sheet was plain and clean.

“Drawing paper!” she cried. “Scribbling paper! Sheets and sheets of it! We can decorate the dolls’ house and paper all the walls. We can also play school.”

James found an old chain. He lifted it and clanked it joyfully.

“A strong, useful chain!” he said. “We can use it when we play prison. I will chain you up and you can try to escape.”

Jenny laughed and picked up a small wooden box. To grown-ups, the dump might have looked messy. To the children, it was full of ideas. They saw toys, games, and make-believe adventures waiting to begin.
