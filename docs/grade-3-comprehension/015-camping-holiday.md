# Grade 3 English Comprehension Exam 015: A Camping Holiday

## Teacher Notes

- This passage has been rewritten and expanded for Grade 3 learners.
- Questions test reading comprehension, vocabulary in context, inference, sequencing, values, and light spelling recognition.
- The questions avoid sounding like grammar drills while still strengthening language skills.

## Passage

Last summer, Roger and his sister Jill went on a camping holiday in Wales with their parents. It was their first camping trip, so everything felt new and exciting.

Their family had a blue tent with two bedrooms and a small living room. They pitched it in a large field near a sandy bay. There were no other tents in the field, so Roger pretended they were brave explorers in a quiet new land.

Every morning, Roger, Jill, and their mother went swimming in the sea. Their father stayed near the tent and cooked breakfast on a portable gas stove. When the swimmers came back, they were hungry and happy. They ate warm beans and eggs together.

After breakfast, the whole family went down to the beach. They played cricket, built shapes in the sand, and enjoyed the sunshine. In the afternoon, Roger and Jill used small nets to look for tiny fish and sea creatures in the clear pools. Their parents sat nearby reading books.

In the evening, the family walked to a farmhouse at the foot of the hill. They watched the cows being milked and had supper with the farmer and his wife. The farmer told them about his animals and crops. He even promised that Roger and Jill could help with the harvest if they came back in autumn.

At sunset, the family strolled back along the quiet road. They climbed into their sleeping bags and fell fast asleep. Nothing woke them until the birds began singing the next morning.
