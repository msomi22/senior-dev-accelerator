# Grade 3 English Comprehension Exam 013: Black Beauty and Ginger

## Teacher Notes

- This passage has been rewritten and expanded for Grade 3 learners.
- Questions test reading comprehension, vocabulary in context, inference, sequencing, values, and light spelling recognition.
- The questions avoid sounding like grammar drills while still strengthening language skills.

## Passage

Black Beauty and Ginger were two strong carriage horses. One morning, their master and mistress decided to visit friends who lived forty-six miles away. James, the careful driver, prepared the carriage and made sure the horses were comfortable before the journey began.

On the first day, they travelled thirty-two miles. The road was not easy. Some parts were smooth, but others had long, steep hills. James drove gently and never hurried the horses. When they went downhill, he put on the brake so the carriage would not push too hard against them. At the right place, he took the brake off again.

James also guided the horses to the smoothest part of the road so their feet would not hurt. When a hill was very long, he turned the wheels a little across the road. This stopped the carriage from rolling backwards and gave Black Beauty and Ginger time to breathe.

James spoke kind words to the horses along the way. To a tired horse, kind words and careful driving can feel as helpful as food and rest.

As the sun was setting, they reached a busy town. They stopped at the biggest hotel in the Market Place. The carriage went under an archway and into a long yard. At the far end of the yard were clean stables where Black Beauty and Ginger could eat, drink, and rest for the night.
