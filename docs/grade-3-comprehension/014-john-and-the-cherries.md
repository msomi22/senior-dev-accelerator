# Grade 3 English Comprehension Exam 014: John and the Cherries

## Teacher Notes

- This passage has been rewritten and expanded for Grade 3 learners.
- Questions test reading comprehension, vocabulary in context, inference, sequencing, values, and light spelling recognition.
- The questions avoid sounding like grammar drills while still strengthening language skills.

## Passage

One Saturday morning, John went shopping with his mother. Their first stop was the greengrocer’s shop. The shop was full of bright fruits and fresh vegetables. John saw apples, bananas, oranges, carrots, and a box of shiny red cherries.

John looked longingly at the cherries. He wanted some very much, but he did not ask for them.

The greengrocer noticed John’s eyes and smiled. “Help yourself to a handful, John,” he said.

John stood still. He did not move his hand towards the box.

“I’m sure you like cherries, don’t you?” asked the puzzled greengrocer.

John nodded quickly. The greengrocer thought John was too shy to take any cherries by himself. So he reached into the box and gave John a large handful.

After they left the shop, John’s mother asked, “Why didn’t you take the cherries when the greengrocer told you to?”

John looked up and answered, “Well, Mummy, his hand is twice as big as mine!”

His mother laughed. John had been quiet, but he had also been very clever.
