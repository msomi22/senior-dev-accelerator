# Grade 3 English Comprehension Exam 020: Raman Meets the Rocking-Horse

## Teacher Notes

- This passage has been rewritten and expanded for Grade 3 learners.
- Questions test reading comprehension, vocabulary in context, inference, sequencing, values, and light spelling recognition.
- The questions avoid sounding like grammar drills while still strengthening language skills.

## Passage

After school, Raman asked his mother if he could go to Jock’s house to play. His mother smiled and said yes. She told him she would come and fetch him at half past five.

Raman’s mother wore a long, flowing sari with bright colours. When Jock saw her, he thought she looked beautiful and royal.

“She is like an Indian princess,” Jock thought. “If she is ever in danger, I will ride to her rescue.”

When the boys reached Jock’s house, Jock led Raman down to the basement room. In the corner stood a large wooden rocking-horse. Jock walked over to it and patted its neck proudly.

“What is he called?” Raman asked, gazing at the rocking-horse with admiration.

Jock paused. He had not yet chosen a name for the horse. But he did not want Raman to know that.

“I know his name,” Jock said quickly, “but it is a secret. He only allows me to call him by it.”

Raman believed him. “He is wonderful,” he whispered. “May I have a ride?”

Jock wanted the first ride himself, so he answered, “Well, he does not like strangers much. I will ride him first so he can look at you and get used to you.”

Raman waited politely. He admired the horse even more. Jock climbed on, rocking gently, and imagined he was riding through a faraway kingdom on a brave secret horse.

### Question 1

B. Before breakfast
C. At midnight

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The passage begins after school.

B. Half past six

**Explanation:** She said half past five.
