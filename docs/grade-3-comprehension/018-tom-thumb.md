# Grade 3 English Comprehension Exam 018: Tom Thumb

## Teacher Notes

- This passage has been rewritten and expanded for Grade 3 learners.
- Questions test reading comprehension, vocabulary in context, inference, sequencing, values, and light spelling recognition.
- The questions avoid sounding like grammar drills while still strengthening language skills.

## Passage

Tom Thumb lived with his parents and brothers near a thick forest. One day, their father, a poor woodman, took the children into the forest to help gather sticks. The trees grew so close together that the children could not see far ahead.

The woodman began cutting wood. The children picked up sticks and tied them into small bundles. While everyone seemed busy, the father and mother slowly moved away. Then, suddenly, they ran through the bushes and disappeared.

When the children noticed they were alone, they began to cry loudly. The forest looked dark and strange. They were afraid they would never find the way home.

Tom Thumb did not cry. Before they entered the forest, he had filled his pockets with small white stones. As they walked, he had quietly dropped the stones one by one along the path.

“Do not be afraid,” Tom told his brothers. “Father and Mother have left us here, but I can take you home. Follow me.”

The children followed Tom. He looked carefully for the white stones shining on the ground. Step by step, the stones led them back along the same path. At last, Tom brought his brothers safely home.

Although Tom was the smallest child, he was brave, calm, and clever.

### Question 1

A. Near a thick forest
B. Beside the sea
C. In a castle
D. In a shop

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** They lived near a thick forest.

### Question 2

A. He was a woodman.
B. He was a farmer.
C. He was a teacher.
D. He was a driver.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The passage calls him a poor woodman.

**Explanation:** They went to gather sticks.
