# Grade 3 English Comprehension Exam 016: The Lion and the Mouse

## Teacher Notes

- This passage has been rewritten and expanded for Grade 3 learners.
- Questions test reading comprehension, vocabulary in context, inference, sequencing, values, and light spelling recognition.
- The questions avoid sounding like grammar drills while still strengthening language skills.

## Passage

One hot afternoon, a lion slept in his den after a long hunt. A playful little mouse ran across the lion’s paw and over his nose. The lion woke up with a roar.

Quickly, he trapped the frightened mouse under his mighty paw.

“Please do not kill me,” squeaked the mouse. “Let me go this time, and one day I may help you.”

The lion laughed softly. “You are very small,” he said. “How could you ever help me, the king of beasts?” Still, the lion felt kind, so he lifted his paw and let the mouse run away.

A few days later, hunters placed a strong rope net in the forest. While the lion was walking among the trees, he stepped into the net and became trapped. He pulled and twisted, but the ropes only grew tighter. The lion roared so loudly that the sound echoed through the forest.

The little mouse heard the roar and knew it was the lion who had spared his life. He ran to the net at once.

“Your Majesty,” said the mouse, “now I can repay your kindness.”

The mouse used his sharp teeth to nibble through the ropes. Bit by bit, the net opened. Soon the lion was free.

The lion looked at the tiny mouse with respect. He had learned that even the smallest friend can be a great helper. Kindness is never wasted.

### Question 1

B. In a shop
C. On a boat
D. In a classroom

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The lion slept in his den.
