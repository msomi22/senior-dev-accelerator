# Grade 3 English Comprehension Exam 017: The Fox and the Goat

## Teacher Notes

- This passage has been rewritten and expanded for Grade 3 learners.
- Questions test reading comprehension, vocabulary in context, inference, sequencing, values, and light spelling recognition.
- The questions avoid sounding like grammar drills while still strengthening language skills.

## Passage

One dry day, a thirsty fox came to a deep well. He leaned over the side to drink the cool water, but he leaned too far and slipped in with a splash.

The fox drank the water, but soon he had a serious problem. The walls of the well were high and smooth. No matter how hard he jumped, he could not climb out.

After a while, a goat came along the path. He looked down and saw the fox in the well.

“Why are you down there?” asked the goat.

The fox was clever and cunning. He did not say, “I am trapped.” Instead, he smiled and said, “I am enjoying the freshest, coolest water. Come down and taste it for yourself.”

The goat was thirsty too. Without stopping to think, he jumped into the well.

As soon as the goat reached the bottom, the fox leaped onto the goat’s back. Then he jumped onto the goat’s horns and scrambled out of the well.

The goat looked up sadly. “What about me?” he asked.

The fox looked down and said, “Next time, friend goat, look before you leap.”

Then the fox ran away, leaving the goat to wait for help. The goat learned that it is wise to think carefully before acting.

## Questions and Answers

### Question 1

Why did the fox go to the well?

A. He was thirsty.
B. He wanted to sleep.
C. He was hiding from rain.
D. He wanted to meet a goat.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The fox was thirsty.

### Question 2

What happened when the fox leaned over?

A. He slipped into the well.
B. He found cherries.
C. He flew away.
D. He saw a horse.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** He leaned too far and slipped in.

### Question 3

Why could the fox not get out?

A. The walls were high and smooth.
B. He was asleep.
C. The water was too sweet.
D. The goat stopped him.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The well walls were high and smooth.

### Question 4

Who came along the path?

A. A goat
B. A lion
C. A mouse
D. A shepherd

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** A goat came along.

### Question 5

What did the goat ask?

A. Why the fox was down there
B. Where the market was
C. What time it was
D. Who owned the cherries

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The goat asked why he was in the well.

### Question 6

Was the fox honest with the goat?

A. No
B. Yes
C. Only at the end
D. The passage does not say

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** He did not say he was trapped.

### Question 7

What did the fox say about the water?

A. It was fresh and cool.
B. It was dirty.
C. It was gone.
D. It was too hot.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** He called it fresh and cool.

### Question 8

Why did the goat jump in?

A. He was thirsty and did not stop to think.
B. He wanted to rescue the fox carefully.
C. He knew the way out.
D. He was pushed.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The goat was thirsty and foolish.

### Question 9

How did the fox escape?

A. He climbed on the goat’s back and horns.
B. He flew out.
C. He dug a tunnel.
D. He used a rope.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** He used the goat to climb out.

### Question 10

What did the goat ask after the fox escaped?

A. What about me?
B. Where is the honey?
C. Can I sleep?
D. Who is James?

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The goat asked, 'What about me?'

### Question 11

What does “cunning” mean?

A. Clever in a tricky way
B. Very sleepy
C. Kind and gentle
D. Full of fear

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The fox tricked the goat.

### Question 12

What does “scrambled out” mean?

A. Climbed out quickly and awkwardly
B. Walked slowly down
C. Slept inside
D. Swam deeper

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** Scrambled means climbed quickly with effort.

**Explanation:** Thirsty is correct.

### Question 14

A. Look before you leap.

**Explanation:** The fox told the goat to look before leaping.

### Question 15

### Question 17
