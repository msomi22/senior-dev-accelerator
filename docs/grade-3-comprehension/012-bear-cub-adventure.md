# Grade 3 English Comprehension Exam 012: A Bear Cub's Adventure

## Teacher Notes

- This passage has been rewritten and expanded for Grade 3 learners.
- Questions test reading comprehension, vocabulary in context, inference, sequencing, values, and light spelling recognition.
- The questions avoid sounding like grammar drills while still strengthening language skills.

## Passage

Mishook was a young bear cub who lived in the forest with his mother and two older cubs. Bears do not see very well from far away, but they have a wonderful sense of smell and very sharp hearing. These senses help them find food and notice danger.

One warm afternoon, a gentle breeze moved through the trees. It carried a sweet smell towards Mishook’s nose. Mishook lifted his head and sniffed. He did not know what the smell was, but his mother knew at once.

“Honey,” she grunted softly.

The whole bear family hurried through the forest. They followed the smell for about a kilometre until they reached an old, hollow tree trunk. Bees had made their home inside it, and the tree was full of golden honey.

The bees saw the bears coming and quickly sounded an alarm. Many bees flew out together, buzzing angrily. They tried to protect their honey by stinging the bears.

Mother Bear and the older cubs kept eating calmly because their thick fur protected them. Mishook pushed his nose too close to the tree. One angry bee stung him right on the nose.

Mishook growled, shook his head, jumped about, snorted, and spun around like a top. At last, he brushed the bee away with his paw. His nose hurt, but the honey tasted so sweet that he soon forgot his trouble. From that day, Mishook knew two things: honey is delicious, and bees do not give it away easily.

## Questions and Answers

### Question 1

Who was Mishook?

A. A young bear cub
B. A farmer
C. A bee
D. A bird

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** Mishook was a young bear cub.

### Question 2

Which two senses are very good in bears?

A. Taste and touch
B. Smell and hearing
C. Sight and taste
D. Touch and sight

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** The passage says bears have a wonderful sense of smell and sharp hearing.

### Question 3

Why are these senses useful to bears?

A. They help bears find food and notice danger.
B. They help bears fly.
C. They help bears write.
D. They help bears sleep all day.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** Good smell and hearing help bears survive.

### Question 4

What carried the sweet smell to Mishook?

A. Rain
B. A gentle breeze
C. A river
D. A bird

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** A gentle breeze moved through the trees and carried the smell.

### Question 5

What was the sweet smell?

A. Milk
B. Honey
C. Flowers only
D. Bread

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Mother Bear knew the smell was honey.

### Question 6

How far did the bears follow the smell?

A. About one kilometre
B. About ten kilometres
C. Across the sea
D. Only one step

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** They followed it for about a kilometre.

### Question 7

Where had the bees made their home?

A. In a hollow tree trunk
B. In a cave
C. Under a stone
D. In a basket

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The bees lived inside an old, hollow tree trunk.

### Question 8

What does “sounded an alarm” mean in the passage?

A. Made a warning signal
B. Played music
C. Went to sleep
D. Opened a door

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The bees warned one another that danger was coming.

### Question 9

Why did the bees sting the bears?

A. To protect their honey
B. To play with them
C. To show the way
D. To thank them

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The bees tried to protect their honey.

### Question 10

Why could Mother Bear continue eating?

A. Her thick fur protected her.
B. The bees liked her.
C. She was hiding in water.
D. She had no nose.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The thick fur protected her from many stings.

### Question 11

Where was Mishook stung?

A. On his paw
B. On his nose
C. On his ear
D. On his back

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** One angry bee stung his nose.

### Question 12

Which action did Mishook do to remove the bee?

A. He brushed it away with his paw.
B. He sang to it.
C. He gave it honey.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** At last, he brushed the bee away with his paw.

### Question 13

Which word in the story means empty inside?

A. hollow
B. golden
C. gentle
D. sharp

**Explanation:** A hollow tree trunk is empty inside.

### Question 14

A. Very tasty
B. Very heavy
C. Very loud
D. Very cold

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The honey tasted sweet and delicious.

### Question 15

A. hony
B. honney
C. honey

**Explanation:** Honey is the correct spelling.

### Question 16

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The ending says bees do not give honey away easily.

B. Hurt and angry
