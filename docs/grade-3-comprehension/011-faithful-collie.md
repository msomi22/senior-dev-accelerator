# Grade 3 English Comprehension Exam 011: The Faithful Collie

## Teacher Notes

- This passage has been rewritten and expanded for Grade 3 learners.
- Questions test reading comprehension, vocabulary in context, inference, sequencing, values, and light spelling recognition.
- The questions avoid sounding like grammar drills while still strengthening language skills.

## Passage

James Hogg was a poet, but he was also a shepherd. One winter evening, he was looking after his sheep on a wide, lonely moor. Suddenly, dark clouds covered the sky and heavy snow began to fall.

James knew he had to bring the sheep home before the storm became worse. He whistled for his faithful collie, Belle. When Belle ran to him, James pointed to one side of the moor and said, “Bring in the sheep from that side. I will gather the sheep from this side.”

James worked for a long time in the freezing snow. At last, he reached his cabin with the sheep he had found. Belle was not there. James felt worried, but he hoped she was still working.

Several hours later, he heard a weak whine and a soft scratching at the door. He rushed outside and saw Belle standing in the snow. She had brought all her sheep home. Not one was missing.

Then James noticed something tiny in Belle’s mouth. Belle came forward and gently laid a newborn puppy at his feet. Before James could speak, Belle turned and went back into the snow. Soon she returned with another puppy. She placed it beside the first one, then sank to the ground.

James knelt beside Belle with tears in his eyes. She had given birth during the storm, yet she had still done her work and saved the sheep. James wrapped the puppies in a warm blanket and carried Belle carefully into the cabin. He never forgot the brave collie who stayed faithful to the end.

## Questions and Answers

### Question 1

What two jobs did James Hogg do?

A. He was a poet and a shepherd.
B. He was a farmer and a builder.
C. He was a teacher and a poet.
D. He was a hunter and a cook.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The first sentence says James Hogg was a poet and also a shepherd.

### Question 2

Where was James when the snow began to fall?

A. In a market
B. On a wide, lonely moor
C. Inside his cabin
D. At a school

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** The passage says he was looking after sheep on a wide, lonely moor.

### Question 3

Why did James need to bring the sheep home quickly?

A. The sheep were hungry.
B. The storm was becoming worse.
C. Belle wanted to rest.
D. The cabin was far away.

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Heavy snow had begun to fall, so the sheep needed safety.

### Question 4

How did James call Belle?

A. He rang a bell.
B. He shouted her name only.
C. He whistled for her.
D. He sent another dog.

**Answer:** C

**Explanation:** The passage says James whistled for his faithful collie.

### Question 5

What job did James give Belle?

A. To guard the cabin
B. To find the puppies
C. To bring in sheep from one side of the moor
D. To cook supper

**Answer:** C

**Explanation:** James told Belle to bring in sheep from one side of the moor.

### Question 6

What did James do on the other side of the moor?

A. He gathered sheep.
B. He slept.
C. He searched for firewood.
D. He followed Belle.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** James said he would gather the sheep from his side.

### Question 7

Why did James feel worried when he reached the cabin?

A. The cabin door was broken.
B. Belle had not returned.
C. The sheep were missing.
D. The snow had stopped.

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Belle was not there when James arrived.

### Question 8

What did James hear after several hours?

A. A loud bark
B. A weak whine and soft scratching
C. A bell ringing
D. A sheep jumping

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** The passage says he heard a weak whine and soft scratching at the door.

### Question 9

What showed that Belle had done her work well?

A. She ate her food quickly.
B. She brought all her sheep home.
C. She slept outside.
D. She barked loudly.

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Not one sheep was missing.

### Question 10

What was Belle carrying in her mouth?

A. A piece of rope
B. A newborn puppy
C. A warm blanket
D. A small sheep

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Belle gently laid a newborn puppy at James’s feet.

### Question 11

Which word in the passage means loyal and trusted?

A. lonely
B. faithful
C. freezing
D. tiny

**Explanation:** Faithful means loyal and trusted.

### Question 12

A. Very old
B. Just born

D. Lost

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** The puppy had just been born.

### Question 13

Why did Belle go back into the snow after bringing the first puppy?

A. To fetch another puppy
B. To look for James
C. To chase a sheep
D. To play

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** She returned with another puppy.

### Question 14

How did James feel when Belle sank to the ground?

A. Angry
B. Careless
C. Sad and thankful
D. Bored

**Answer:** C

**Explanation:** He knelt beside her with tears in his eyes.

### Question 15

A. Hot and sunny
B. Windy and dry
C. Snowy and freezing
D. Warm and cloudy

**Answer:** C

**Explanation:** The passage describes heavy snow and freezing weather.

### Question 16

A. sheperd

### Question 17

A. Being faithful and responsible is important.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** Belle stayed faithful and responsible even in danger.

**Explanation:** Belle is brave and faithful.

A. To keep them warm and safe
B. To hide them
C. To carry sheep
D. To clean the floor

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** Newborn puppies need warmth, especially after a snowstorm.

### Question 20

A. selfish
B. brave
C. forgetful
D. playful only

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Belle worked bravely during the storm and protected the sheep and puppies.
